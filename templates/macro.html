USD/MXN Analysis

Structural drivers of the peso

The USD/MXN has experienced high volatility since 2020, ranging from $17.08 (best 2024) to $24.83 (COVID peak 2020). The peso is LATAM's most traded currency, with a daily average volume of over USD 114 billion.

Positive real interest rates in Mexico attract foreign capital, supporting the peso. The nearshoring effect has generated structural demand for MXN to pay wages and construction, creating a counterbalancing force against depreciation pressures.

Current levels (2026): Banxico rate 9.0% · Fed Funds 4.50% · Spread +450 bps · USD/MXN spot ~18.80
Monetary Policy

Banxico vs. Fed: The Rate Differential

The gap between Banxico and Fed rates determines capital flows into Mexico. When this differential narrows, investors move funds to the U.S., pressuring the peso to depreciate.

The IMF projects Mexico GDP growth of 1.2% for 2026, below the 2024 pace. U.S. inflation is converging toward the 2% target, while Mexico inflation remains at 3.8% (INPC March 2026).

Nearshoring

The Manufacturing Relocation Boom

Nearshoring has generated an investment boom in Mexico, with companies like Tesla, TSMC, BMW and hundreds of automotive suppliers relocating production from Asia to Mexico.

These plants require MDF and MDP boards for industrial construction, furniture and interior fit-out, generating estimated additional demand of 800,000 m³/year — directly benefiting Arauco as the #1 panel supplier in Mexico.

FDI Mexico 2022–2025: Over USD 212 billion in announced nearshoring investments. Arauco Proyecto Vikingo ($300M) is positioned to capture this structural demand growth. (Secretaría de Economía, 2025)

Macroeconomic Scenarios

What Could Happen with USD/MXN?

Three forward-looking scenarios for the USD/MXN exchange rate and their impact on Arauco Mexico margins.

🟢 Optimistic Scenario
16.00 – 17.00

The Fed aggressively cuts rates. Banxico–Fed spread widens. Nearshoring maintains USD flows to Mexico. MXN appreciates. For Arauco: Lower USD input costs → improved operating margins in Mexico.

⚪ Base Scenario
17.50 – 19.50

Relative stability with moderate volatility. Banxico maintains positive differential. Local political uncertainty offset by nearshoring flows. For Arauco: Stable margins, active cost management required.

🔴 Adverse Scenario
20.00 – 23.00

Political or economic crisis in Mexico, U.S. inflationary rebound, emerging market capital outflows. MXN depreciates sharply. For Arauco: MXN input costs spike, significant margin compression.
