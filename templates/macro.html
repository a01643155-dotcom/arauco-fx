Current Situation · H1 2026

Easing cycle underway amid cautious optimism

During the first half of 2026, Mexico's economy showed clear signs of slowing, with economic activity contracting during Q1. The weakening growth environment has eased domestic demand pressures and allowed Banxico to continue its monetary easing cycle, lowering the benchmark rate from 7.00% to 6.50% between March and May.

While inflation has continued its gradual decline, reaching approximately 4.5%, it remains above the central bank's long-term target of 3%. Policymakers have maintained a cautious approach to monetary policy despite the recent rate cuts.

Despite global headwinds — geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, potential shifts in U.S. economic policy, and volatility in global commodity markets — the Mexican peso has remained relatively resilient and recently appreciated against the U.S. dollar, helping to alleviate some inflationary pressures.

Implication for Arauco: A stronger MXN reduces the MXN cost of USD-denominated inputs (urea, methanol, resins). The ongoing rate easing cycle may further support the peso in the near term, temporarily improving margins at Mexican plants.

U.S. Economy — H1 2026

America's Slowdown Reshapes the Bilateral Equation

The United States is the origin of all USD-denominated raw material costs for Arauco Mexico — and the destination of its timber exports. U.S. monetary policy, inflation, and trade decisions directly affect input margins in Durango and Cuautitlán.

Three forces Arauco must monitor

1. Inflation rebound driven by energy. U.S. CPI accelerated to 3.8% in April 2026 — the highest reading since May 2023 — as energy prices surged 17.9% year-on-year amid the conflict in the Middle East. This directly pressures Arauco: urea and methanol, both energy-intensive petrochemicals quoted in USD, are rising in cost alongside oil.

2. The Fed is stuck on hold. With inflation well above the 2% target, the Federal Reserve has kept rates at 3.50%–3.75% for three consecutive meetings. The April 29 decision saw an unusual 8-4 dissenting vote, reflecting internal tension between fighting inflation and supporting a slowing economy (Q1 GDP revised down to +1.6%). No rate cut is expected at the June 16–17 FOMC meeting.

3. The Banxico–Fed spread is narrowing fast. In 2023, Mexico's rate advantage over the U.S. was ~6 percentage points — a powerful magnet for capital inflows and a key support for the peso. By H1 2026, that spread has compressed to ~2.75 pp (6.50% vs. 3.75%), reducing the carry-trade incentive that historically underpinned MXN strength.

Nearshoring Effect

The structural demand driver for Arauco

Nearshoring has generated an investment boom in Mexico, with companies like Tesla, TSMC, BMW and hundreds of automotive suppliers relocating production from Asia to Mexico. These plants require MDF and MDP boards for industrial construction.

Estimated additional panel board demand: 800,000 m³/year — directly benefiting Arauco as the #1 panel supplier. FDI announcements 2022–2025 exceeded USD 212 billion. (Secretaría de Economía, 2025)
