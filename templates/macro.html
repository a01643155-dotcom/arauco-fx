USD/MXN Analysis

The peso: between strength and volatility

The MXN experienced an unusually strong period (2022–2024) driven by the Banxico–Fed rate differential (carry trade) and the nearshoring boom. The exchange rate reached 16.62 MXN/USD in May 2023, a multi-year historic low.

However, factors such as post-election political uncertainty (2024), US economic slowdown and normalization of the rate differential pushed the exchange rate to 20.20 by end-2024, representing a ~22% depreciation in just months.

Relevance for Arauco: MXN volatility directly impacts the cost of dollar-denominated raw materials (urea, methanol, phenol) that Arauco pays for its Mexican plants. A 10% MXN depreciation can increase COGS in MXN by 3.5% to 5.5% depending on the production mix.
Monetary Policy

Rate Differential: The Carry Trade Engine

Interest Rate Parity (IRP) establishes that the forward exchange rate incorporates the differential between domestic and foreign rates. With Banxico at ~8.5% and the Fed at ~4.5%, the ~400 basis point spread keeps the MXN relatively attractive for carry investors.

Nearshoring

The big post-pandemic opportunity for Mexico

Nearshoring has attracted more than US$35B in foreign direct investment to Mexico between 2022-2025 (Ministry of Economy, 2025). Semiconductor, automotive and appliance companies are building or expanding plants in Monterrey, Guadalajara, Guanajuato and Querétaro.

Every new factory demands MDF and MDP boards for offices, interiors and industrial furniture. Arauco estimates an additional demand increase of 15-20% in the industrial segment driven by this phenomenon in the 2024-2028 period.

Macroeconomic Scenarios

What could happen with USD/MXN?

🟢 Optimistic Scenario
16.00 – 17.00

The Fed cuts rates aggressively. The Banxico–Fed differential widens. Nearshoring maintains USD flows to Mexico. MXN appreciates. For Arauco: Lower USD input costs → better margins in Mexico.

⚪ Base Scenario
17.50 – 19.50

Relative stability with moderate volatility. Banxico maintains positive differential. Local political uncertainty offset by nearshoring. For Arauco: Stable margins, active cost management required.

🔴 Adverse Scenario
20.00 – 23.00

Political or economic crisis in Mexico, inflationary resurgence in the US, capital outflows from emerging markets. MXN depreciates sharply. For Arauco: Input costs in MXN spike, significant margin compression.
