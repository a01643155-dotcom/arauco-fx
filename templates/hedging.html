Why doesn't Arauco use derivatives?

The most common reasons for similar companies not using derivatives include:

  • Accounting complexity (IFRS 9 — hedge accounting)
  • Option premium cost vs. operating margin
  • MXN derivatives market less liquid than USA/Europe
  • Natural hedging considered sufficient by management
  • Internal technical capacity for active derivatives management

However, with Project Vikingo ($300M USD) and new installed capacity in 2026, net USD exposure will grow significantly, which could justify implementing a formal hedging policy with derivatives.

Natural Hedge — How It Works

Implementation Mechanics

Natural hedging is not just a concept — it requires deliberate operational decisions. Here is how Arauco Mexico implements it in practice.

Step 1 — Map USD Flows
USD INFLOWS — Natural hedge source
Timber exports to North America

38% of Arauco timber sales go to the USA and Canada, generating ~US$133M/year of USD revenues that never need to be converted to MXN. These are invoiced in USD and collected in USD bank accounts in Mexico.

USD OUTFLOWS — Exposure to hedge
Resin · Methanol · Phenol imports

55% of COGS is USD-denominated: chemical inputs (resin, methanol, phenol) purchased from global suppliers at ~US$120M/year, invoiced in USD.

Step 2 — Match & Net
Natural Offset Calculation
USD inflows (timber exports) +US$133M
USD outflows (inputs) −US$120M
Net residual exposure ~US$13–30M

Timing mismatches (export collections vs. input payment cycles) generate a practical residual of US$30–60M that requires financial instruments.
