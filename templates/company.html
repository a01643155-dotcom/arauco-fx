Corporate Structure

Grupo Angelini at the helm

Arauco is a subsidiary of Grupo Angelini, one of Chile's most important conglomerates. The control chain: Angelini (75.17%) → AntarChile → Empresas Copec (99.99% of Arauco). This structure concentrates decision-making power and enables preferential financing.

With over 50 years of history, Arauco began in southern Chile managing renewable forest resources. Today it operates across four major segments: pulp, wood, panels and energy — maximizing the integral value of every tree.

Variable Cost Structure — MDF Mexico

The primary input for MDF is UF resin (urea-formaldehyde), priced in USD. Urea accounts for 45–55% of resin costs, followed by formaldehyde (25–30%) and methanol (8–12%). USD/MXN exchange rate fluctuations directly impact the operating margin.
