Corporate Structure

Grupo Angelini at the helm

Arauco is a subsidiary of Grupo Angelini, one of Chile's most important conglomerates. The ownership chain is: Angelini (75.17%) → AntarChile → Empresas Copec (99.99% of Arauco). This structure concentrates decision-making power and enables preferential financing.

With more than 50 years of history, Arauco began in southern Chile managing renewable forest resources. Today it operates across four major segments: pulp, timber, panels and energy, maximizing the full value of every tree.

Variable Cost Structure — MDF Mexico

The primary input for MDF is UF resin (urea-formaldehyde), priced in USD. Urea represents 45-55% of resin cost, followed by formaldehyde (25-30%) and methanol (8-12%). Fluctuations in the USD/MXN exchange rate directly impact the operating margin.
