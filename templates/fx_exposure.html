Quantification of net exposure, scenario-based sensitivity analysis and its impact on margins, costs and operating profitability.

The Core Exposure: USD Costs, MXN Revenue

Arauco Mexican plants (Durango, Cuautitlán, Zitácuaro) sell primarily in MXN to the domestic market, but their critical inputs — urea, methanol, phenol, UF resins — are denominated in US dollars. This creates a short USD operating exposure. MXN depreciation directly impacts the gross margin.

📌 Conclusion: Net Operating Exposure

Arauco Mexico has an estimated short USD operating exposure of US$120–150M annually (USD component of COGS at Mexican plants). This means that for every 10 cents of peso depreciation (e.g., from $17 to $17.10/USD), Arauco incurs ~US$800K–1M in additional annual operating cost, all else equal. Translation exposure additionally amounts to the USD value of fixed assets in Mexico (~US$600–800M at book value estimates).
